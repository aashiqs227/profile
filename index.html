How I Work

AI as a Force Multiplier

AI is a cognitive tool, not a magic button. My workflow treats it as a system — structured inputs produce structured outputs.

1

Context First

Prompts are drafted in a Notes app — full situation, task definition, and constraints loaded before the AI sees anything. No lazy inputs.

2

Smartest Models

Always use the most capable model available. The gap between free-tier and frontier isn't incremental — it's generational. The gap compounds.

3

Expertise Layer

AI supplements, it doesn't replace. Domain knowledge shapes the prompt, evaluates the output, and makes the final call. That's where above-average happens.

Anyone not using AI — or not using the most capable models of the time — will fall behind. That dynamic is now best understood as leverage. Those who adapt and implement AI into their workflows gain compounding advantages. In marketing, this leverage shows up across every layer: content creation, campaign planning, automating repetitive operations, and increasingly, the complete management and optimization of paid campaigns. The question isn't whether to use AI — it's how fast you can build the systems around it.
